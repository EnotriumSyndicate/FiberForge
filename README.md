# FiberForge – ENOTRIUM Advanced Kenaf Fiber & Bio-Chemistry Platform

**"From American soil to defense-grade composites and EV materials — AI-orchestrated, vertically integrated."**

FiberForge is the proprietary processing and advanced-materials engine of the Enotrium bioeconomy. It converts industrial hemp bast fiber and bamboo into high-performance tiers for automotive, defense, construction, bioplastics, and battery anodes — all under the **Enotrium AIP** (Artificial Intelligence Platform) orchestration layer.

### Technical AI-Defined Architecture for Hemp Fiber

Enotrium AIP serves as the **autonomous intelligence layer** that orchestrates every stage of FiberForge in real time. The architecture is modular, edge-deployable, and privacy-preserving (zero-knowledge proofs for farmer data).

#### Core AI-Orchestrated Pipeline (4–5 Year Commercialization Horizon)

1. **Input & Decortication (AIP Edge Nodes)**  
   - Real-time hyperspectral grading of incoming hemp bales (Icarus integration)  
   - Dynamic decortication parameter optimization (fiber length 15–50 mm, tensile 550–900 MPa)

2. **Polymer Replacement**  
   - Closed-loop solvent-free extraction using AIP-optimized temperature/pressure cycles  
   - Lignin converted into 100 % bio-based polymer binder (eliminates petroleum binders in battery anodes and bioplastics)  
   - Yield target: >85 % purity with <5 % energy intensity of traditional Kraft process

3. **Carbon Integration with Bamboo-Derived Silicon Additives**  
   - Pyrolysis of hemp hurd → high-surface-area activated carbon anodes (589–1,950 m²/g)  
   - In-line bamboo silicon doping during activation (AIP-controlled CVD-style reactor)  
   - Result: anode capacity 800–2,200 mAh/g (Phase 3 target) with superior cycle life (1,500–3,000 cycles)

4. **Advanced Activation & Surface Modification**  
   - Plasma / microwave / enzymatic surface treatments orchestrated by AIP predictive models  
   - Functionalization for epoxy adhesion (+60 % tensile, +80 % modulus, +7.5× Izod impact)  
   - Ballistic performance: 30 vol% hemp-fabric/epoxy absorbs 95–108.5 J (.22-cal) — 2.6–3× prior natural-fiber composites

5. **Next-Generation Chemistry for High-Margin Applications**  
   - Output streams:  
     • Industrial bast fiber (automotive composites, geotextiles)  
     • Textile-grade fiber  
     • Hemp/bamboo bioplastics (PLA/PHA 60-80 % blends)  
     • Hempcrete aggregate & particleboard  
     • Battery-grade hemp carbon + lignin anodes  
     • Ballistic composites (Berry Amendment compliant)
